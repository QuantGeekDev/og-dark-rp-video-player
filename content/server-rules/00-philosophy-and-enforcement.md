# Philosophy and Enforcement

## In One Breath

DarkRP is allowed to be messy, risky, loud, profitable, unlucky, and funny. It is not allowed to become one person's hobby of ruining everyone else's night.

## Rules

### PHIL-001 - Staff enforce intent, not loopholes

If you act in bad faith, "the exact sentence did not say this exact stunt" will not save you.

Examples:

- You may not use a physics prop to do the same thing as prop blocking just because it is technically a vehicle.
- You may not use a job switch, disconnect, pocket action, or spawn area to escape a scene you are clearly part of.
- You may not invent a mayor law that overrides server rules.

### PHIL-002 - The game systems are the first referee

When OG Dark RP has a system for something, use it. Contracts use the hitman system. Warrants use the law system. Organization benefits use organization permissions. Pocket storage uses the pocket system.

### PHIL-003 - Crime is gameplay, griefing is not

Raiding, pickpocketing, arrests, warrants, stolen printers, hit contracts, bank robberies, and police chases are all part of the server.

The line gets crossed when the goal stops being roleplay conflict and becomes harassment, spawn disruption, repeated targeting without RP reason, or exploiting mechanics.

### PHIL-004 - Punishments should fit the abuse

Staff may use warnings, kicks, bans, job restrictions, tool restrictions, mutes, cleanup, refunds, or targeted access blocks.

If someone abuses props, losing prop access makes sense. If someone abuses law radio, a mute or job restriction makes sense. If someone is here only to break the server, the door is over there and it has a very firm lock.

### PHIL-005 - Accidents and patterns are different

One bad grenade, one confused door breach, or one accidental crossfire death is not the same as a pattern of rule abuse.

Staff should look at intent, repetition, damage caused, evidence, and whether the player corrected their behavior.

### PHIL-006 - Evidence matters

Video is best. Screenshots, timestamps, player names, Steam IDs, and exact mechanic names also help.

If a report cannot be understood or verified, staff may decline it. "He broke rules" is not evidence. "At 12:40, during a bank robbery, X killed me at spawn after I respawned" is useful.

### PHIL-007 - Do not publicize exploits

Report exploits privately through staff/support channels. Do not post steps in OOC, do not demonstrate them for a crowd, and do not farm rewards from them while calling it "testing."

### PHIL-008 - Staff are not a shortcut around normal losses

Losing a legal raid, being arrested for a valid wanted, having a printer stolen, or dying with wallet cash is not automatically a staff issue.

Ask for help when something is abusive, broken, exploitative, or impossible to resolve through gameplay.

## Staff Notes

- Prefer correcting behavior before removing players when the issue is minor.
- Prefer targeted restrictions when one mechanic is being abused.
- Keep staff action separate from in-character revenge.
- Use audit logs and server systems before relying only on chat arguments.
