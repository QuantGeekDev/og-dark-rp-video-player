# Combat, Damage, and Custody

## In One Breath

Weapons are tools for RP conflict. They are not conversation openers, crowd control toys, or punctuation marks.

## Rules

### COMBAT-001 - Every attack needs a valid reason

Before damaging another player, you need a valid RP reason that exists right now.

Valid reasons include active self-defense, valid base defense, law enforcement action, system hit contract, active robbery, active raid, or another clearly connected RP conflict.

### COMBAT-002 - Crossfire is not a shield for reckless combat

Accidents happen. Repeated reckless shots into crowds, explosives in busy streets, and wild vehicle attacks are not protected by "crossfire."

If you choose a dangerous weapon in public, you own the risk of what it hits.

### COMBAT-003 - Explosives require extra care

Grenades, RPGs, satchels, fire, and other area damage must have a valid target and a reasonable combat context.

Do not use explosives to clear sidewalks, punish insults, test damage near spawn, or destroy random property.

### COMBAT-004 - Police tools are for police work

Tasers, cuffs, arrest sticks, unarrest tools, weapon checkers, and battering rams must be used for valid law enforcement scenes.

Using a non-lethal tool randomly is still abuse.

### COMBAT-005 - Tasers are not spam weapons

Tasers briefly disable a target and have recharge rules. Do not tase people for jokes, movement control, harassment, or because you lost an argument.

Friendly law roles and the mayor are protected by friendly-fire rules. Do not try to bypass that protection.

### COMBAT-006 - Handcuffs and restraints need legal reason

Do not restrain players randomly. Use cuffs for arrests, valid warrants, active police scenes, or other server-approved law interactions.

Restrained players must not be used as props, shields, bait, or furniture.

### COMBAT-007 - Arrests require wanted status or clear law authority

Arrest players for valid wanted status, active arrest warrants, or clear law enforcement reasons supported by city laws and server rules.

Random arrests, mass arrests, and arresting people for job names are not allowed.

### COMBAT-008 - Weapon checks must be reasonable

Law enforcement may inspect weapons when the server flow and job authority allow it.

Weapon checks are not an excuse to harass one player forever, search every passerby for fun, or create a fight where none existed.

### COMBAT-009 - Custody is a consequence, not a side quest

Arrested players are under custody restrictions. Do not use alternate accounts, PMs, radio, external voice, or friends to keep acting as if you were free.

Do not camp jail or custody exits for harassment.

### COMBAT-010 - Do not exploit NLR combat fences

The NLR system can block item use, damage, shots, and explosions near a player's death area. Do not search for geometry tricks or projectile paths to bypass it.

If the system blocks your shot, the answer is "leave," not "try a stranger angle."

### COMBAT-011 - Combat logging is not allowed

Do not disconnect, job switch, respawn, or hide in menus to avoid death, arrest, robbery, raid loss, hit failure, or stolen property.

### COMBAT-012 - Public combat should stay connected to a scene

Street fights can happen. Permanent deathmatch blocks cannot.

If the same conflict has stopped making RP sense, move on or staff may end it for you.

## Examples

- Allowed: a CP officer tases a wanted suspect who is fleeing an arrest.
- Not allowed: a CP officer tases a hobo because the hobo looked at the police car.
- Allowed: a base owner shoots a thief who is actively lockpicking their owned door.
- Not allowed: a base owner kills someone across the street because they "looked suspicious."
