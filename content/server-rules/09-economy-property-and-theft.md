# Economy, Property, and Theft

## In One Breath

If it prints, grows, mines, stores, burns, explodes, or glows suspiciously, someone will want it. Protect it, insure it with good decisions, and do not cry "admin" at every crowbar.

## Rules

### ECON-001 - Wallet cash is risky

Your wallet can be spent, taxed in some flows, dropped, stolen by systems, and partially lost on death.

Bank balance is safer than wallet cash. Carrying huge stacks is a choice.

### ECON-002 - Death drops are part of the game

Death can drop wallet cash under the death money rules. Do not report a normal death drop as theft unless the death itself broke rules.

### ECON-003 - Owned does not always mean untouchable

Some owned entities can still be stolen, destroyed, raided, or physically moved by valid roleplay.

Examples:

- Printers can be attacked or stolen through raids.
- Printer radiants can become stealable after destruction.
- Bank gold is stolen property during robbery.
- Vehicles can be lockpicked or breached when rules allow.

### ECON-004 - Owner-only UI does not protect the physical object

Many machines only let owners or admins use the management console. That does not always stop other players from stealing, damaging, discovering, or raiding the machine.

Build like the object matters.

### ECON-005 - Money printers are illegal-risk production

Money printers and printer vaults are high-value contraband unless city law or future server policy says otherwise.

Owners may manage their printers. Raiders and police may interact with them through valid raid, warrant, or contraband scenes.

### ECON-006 - Printer vaults are not rule shields

A printer vault stores printers and can keep them producing. It does not make stolen printers sacred, hide illegal value from valid searches, or protect against staff action for exploits.

### ECON-007 - Bitcoin miners follow bitcoin policy

Bitcoin miners require the miner and generator flow and are restricted for law enforcement jobs.

Do not exploit disconnect liquidation, price display, generator range, or upgrade part merging.

### ECON-008 - Drug product and weed product are contraband-risk items

Drug machines, weed grows, packaged drugs, and related pocket items may attract police and thieves.

Use supported sell, pocket, and drop flows. Do not fake product sales through chat when the system has a real item flow.

### ECON-009 - Pocket storage is not exploit storage

Use the pocket system for supported items only. Do not use pocket timing, drop timing, disconnects, or invalid targets to hide evidence, duplicate items, avoid raids, or escape active RP.

### ECON-010 - Shipments must be used through shipment flow

Weapon and ammo shipments are physical items. They cannot be treated as magic inventory claims unless the system says so.

Do not use shipments to self-supply through job abuse.

### ECON-011 - Radiants and permanent rewards follow their system rules

Radiants, permanent weapon unlocks, lootbox rewards, fragments, keys, cases, and locker rewards must use official UI and item flows.

Duplicate permanents, tax on cash rewards, and claim rules are system outcomes, not staff negotiation points.

### ECON-012 - No economy exploits

Do not duplicate money, farm broken sell loops, bypass shop costs, avoid taxes through bugs, create impossible refunds, or force machines into invalid states for profit.

Report broken loops privately.

### ECON-013 - Scams are limited by official systems and staff judgement

In-character risk is allowed, but staff may act on scams that abuse official systems, real-money promises, impersonation, exploit knowledge, harassment, or deliberate OOC deception.

If a future trade system exists, use it.

### ECON-014 - Taxes are city policy, not theft

City taxes can affect salary, shop purchases, sell paths, lottery winnings, and some rewards. The mayor's use of tax is audited and bounded by server settings.

Complaining about taxes is legal roleplay. Randomly killing the mayor over a 1 percent change still needs a valid RP path.

### ECON-015 - Do not destroy value only to avoid losing it

If a raid, arrest, search, or robbery is active, do not intentionally destroy, despawn, pocket through exploit, disconnect with, or hide valuables only to deny the other side a valid win.

## Practical Advice

- Bank money you do not want to risk.
- Do not leave upgraded machines unguarded.
- Do not put illegal production behind a building sign.
- Use official trade and shop systems whenever possible.
