# Vehicles, Streets, and Transport

## In One Breath

Cars are doors with engines and consequences. Drive like the city has people in it, because it does, and most of them are carrying something expensive.

## Rules

### VEH-001 - Vehicle damage needs RP reason

Do not intentionally run players over, ram cars, block people, or damage vehicles without a valid RP reason.

Vehicle deathmatch is RDM with wheels.

### VEH-002 - Accidents happen, patterns get handled

One awkward bump is not the same as repeated ramming, sidewalk hunting, or camping spawn roads.

Slow down near crowds, NPCs, shops, spawn, and active staff scenes.

### VEH-003 - Do not block required routes

Do not park or abandon vehicles in spawn exits, NPC areas, bank entrances, police entrances, shop counters, narrow tunnels, or roads needed for normal traffic.

Staff may move or delete blocking vehicles.

### VEH-004 - Vehicle locks follow access rules

Vehicles use owner, co-owner, organization, shared access, and lock state. If a vehicle is locked, use keys, valid access, lockpick, or law tools as appropriate.

Do not claim "it was mine" while bypassing the lock system through exploits.

### VEH-005 - Vehicle theft must use valid methods

Stealing a vehicle should involve lockpicking, valid access, owner mistake, or another supported system path.

Do not steal through physics abuse, seating bugs, teleporting, or prop displacement.

### VEH-006 - Refueling must respect ownership and locks

Use fuel can and refuel flows as intended. Do not grief by draining, blocking, or repeatedly interfering with refuels.

Locked vehicles may deny refuel unless you have access or are an occupant.

### VEH-007 - Drive-by combat follows combat rules

Passenger shooting or vehicle combat still needs valid RP reason. Being in a moving vehicle does not create permission to fire at everyone.

Drivers and passengers share responsibility for obvious combat abuse.

### VEH-008 - Do not use vehicles as mobile props

Do not use cars to body block, force players into corners, push props, wedge doors, make barricades during raids, or trap NPCs.

Use a base if you want a wall. Use a car if you want to go somewhere.

### VEH-009 - Physgun limits are strict

Do not physgun occupied vehicles. Do not freeze vehicles. Do not use the physgun to move vehicles during active fights, raids, arrests, or chases.

The code blocks some of this. Staff may handle the rest.

### VEH-010 - Police vehicle action needs law context

Law enforcement may stop, search, breach, or interfere with vehicles when warrants, wanted status, observed crimes, or valid law scenes justify it.

Do not use police tools to randomly harass parked cars.

### VEH-011 - Crashes are not automatic kill permission

A crash, bump, or accidental collision does not automatically let everyone involved start a gunfight.

Escalate only when the context supports it, such as deliberate ramming during a chase.

### VEH-012 - Keep dealerships and spawn points clear

Do not camp dealer spawn points, trap newly spawned vehicles, or block car dealer NPCs.

## Examples

- Allowed: police ram a suspect car during an active chase.
- Not allowed: a player repeatedly drives into pedestrians outside spawn.
- Allowed: a thief lockpicks a car during a theft scene.
- Not allowed: using physgun to drag a truck with a driver out of a base.
