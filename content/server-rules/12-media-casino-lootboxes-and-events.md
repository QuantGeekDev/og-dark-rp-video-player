# Media, Casino, Lootboxes, and Events

## In One Breath

The city may have screens, slots, cases, and questionable luck. It does not have a license for ear damage, fake payouts, or "trust me bro" economics.

## Rules

### MEDIA-001 - Media content must follow conduct rules

TV, cinema screens, direct video panels, audio, signage, and public displays cannot contain hate speech, harassment, pornography, gore spam, extremist content, exploit instructions, or staff impersonation.

If it would get removed as a sign, it can get removed as a video.

### MEDIA-002 - Keep public media volume reasonable

Do not use cinema or TV systems to drown out normal play, spam audio, follow players with noise, or harass specific areas.

Cinema Owner and related roles get tools to create scenes, not to win the volume war.

### MEDIA-003 - Do not fake official casino systems

Slots, poker, coinflip, and future casino games must use the official mechanics when they exist.

Do not create fake UI, fake payouts, fake odds, or manual games that players can confuse with server-backed systems.

### MEDIA-004 - Gambling disputes follow the system record

When a casino or coinflip system records the outcome, that outcome stands unless there is a bug.

Bad luck is not a bug. It is just bad luck wearing a nicer jacket.

### MEDIA-005 - Lootboxes use official case and key flow

Cases, keys, fragments, locker rewards, permanent unlocks, duplicate conversion, and taxable cash rewards follow the lootbox system.

Do not sell fake keys, fake case odds, fake locker claims, or fake permanent unlocks.

### MEDIA-006 - Radiants are special items, not admin promises

Radiants follow their item, pocket, install, removal, and theft rules. If a radiant drops after a machine explosion and becomes stealable, that is part of the risk unless a bug caused it.

### MEDIA-007 - Do not exploit minigames

Do not use timing bugs, disconnects, UI glitches, packet delay, duplication, invalid seating, or collusion exploits to force casino, lootbox, or media outcomes.

Report bugs privately.

### MEDIA-008 - Events must announce exceptions clearly

If staff or a future event system runs an event that changes normal rules, the exception must be clear, time-limited, and announced.

No one gets to declare their own purge because Tuesday felt long.

### MEDIA-009 - Event rules end when the event ends

Special event permissions do not carry into normal play. When the event ends, normal RDM, NLR, job, building, and conduct rules return immediately.

### MEDIA-010 - Public contests need clear terms

Player-run raffles, shows, races, tournaments, or contests must clearly state prizes, entry cost, and rules before collecting money.

Staff may decline to enforce informal events that were vague, risky, or outside official systems.

## Examples

- Allowed: a cinema night with reasonable volume and clean content.
- Not allowed: looping offensive clips on a public TV.
- Allowed: using official coinflip.
- Not allowed: claiming your prop table is the "real server casino" and refusing payouts.
