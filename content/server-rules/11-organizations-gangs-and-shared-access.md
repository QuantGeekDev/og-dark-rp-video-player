# Organizations, Gangs, and Shared Access

## In One Breath

An organization is a crew, not a magic excuse. It gives shared identity and access, and it also gives staff a nice tidy thread to pull when the whole crew acts silly.

## Rules

### ORG-001 - Organization systems define organization benefits

Use the organization system for tags, ranks, permissions, treasury, invites, shared access, upgrades, and group identity.

Do not claim organization benefits that the system does not grant.

### ORG-002 - Do not impersonate organizations

Do not copy another organization's name, tag, colors, rank titles, or identity to mislead players.

Parody is fine when it is obvious. Fraud is not.

### ORG-003 - Shared access is real responsibility

If you grant someone door, vehicle, base, or organization access, they may be able to use that access in meaningful ways.

Choose co-owners and org permissions carefully. Staff will not always undo a bad trust decision.

### ORG-004 - Shared bases need clear membership

Defending a shared base requires real connection: ownership, co-ownership, organization membership, basing together, or active involvement in the scene.

"He is my friend" is not enough by itself.

### ORG-005 - Organization membership can justify some assists

Some systems allow same-organization assistance, such as eligible gangsters and mob bosses assisting specific bank robberies.

If the code requires organization linkage, chat claims do not replace it.

### ORG-006 - Leaders must manage treasury honestly

Organization treasury, upgrades, withdrawals, and rank permissions are serious group assets.

Do not use leadership roles to steal through exploit, impersonation, or hidden rule abuse. Ordinary bad leadership is a group problem; malicious abuse may become a staff problem.

### ORG-007 - Organization chat is not metagame immunity

Group chat and external voice still follow metagaming rules during active scenes.

Do not use out-of-character information to coordinate raids, police response, revenge, or NLR violations.

### ORG-008 - Organizations do not override jobs

Your organization does not grant police powers, mayor authority, hitman contracts, shop access, or criminal tools unless your job and systems also allow it.

A "police gang" without police jobs is just people with opinions.

### ORG-009 - Crew responsibility can scale with coordination

Staff may treat coordinated abuse as a group issue when multiple members knowingly participate.

One member breaking a rule does not automatically punish everyone, but a planned crew-wide exploit or harassment campaign can.

### ORG-010 - Wars and rivalries need scenes

Gang rivalries are allowed. Permanent random killing under "we are at war" is not.

Create raids, turf disputes, robberies, contracts, negotiations, and threats that players can understand.

## Practical Advice

- Keep ranks tight.
- Audit treasury actions.
- Grant access only to people you trust.
- Do not let one reckless member turn your base into a staff landmark.
