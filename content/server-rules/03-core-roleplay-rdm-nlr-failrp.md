# Core Roleplay, RDM, NLR, and FailRP

## In One Breath

Have a reason before you hurt someone, respect the new life after death, and do not use the menu, spawn, or job system as an eject button from consequences.

## Rules

### CORE-001 - RDM is not allowed

RDM means killing or trying to kill another player without a valid roleplay reason.

Valid reasons can include self-defense, active raids, wanted arrests, hit contracts, bank robbery conflict, defending your owned property, or another clear system-backed RP situation.

"I was bored" is not a reason. "They annoyed me in OOC" is worse.

### CORE-002 - ARDM is still a rule break

Attempted random deathmatch counts even if the target survives.

Shooting near someone, throwing explosives at them, ramming them, or repeatedly damaging them without a valid reason is not made okay because your aim was bad.

### CORE-003 - Self-defense starts with real threat or damage

You may defend yourself when someone is actively attacking you, breaking into your owned base, trying to restrain you unlawfully, or otherwise creating an immediate threat.

Do not stretch self-defense into revenge. If the scene is over and everyone has left, start a new scene or move on.

### CORE-004 - New Life Rule applies after death

When you die, your new life should not use revenge knowledge from the previous one.

The server creates personal NLR zones that can block item use, damage, shots, and explosions near your death area. Even when the mechanical timer clears, do not rejoin the same active conflict just to swing the outcome.

### CORE-005 - Do not return to or influence your death scene

While NLR is active, do not return to the area, shoot into it, throw explosives into it, send friends tactical details, or use outside knowledge to continue the fight.

If you died defending a base or robbery, you lost that life. Let the scene resolve.

### CORE-006 - Revenge killing is not allowed

Someone killing you does not automatically give your next life permission to kill them back.

Report repeated abuse. Do not create a revenge loop that keeps the whole street permanently on fire.

### CORE-007 - FailRP means abusing the role or scene

FailRP is acting against the basic roleplay structure of your job, scene, or active system.

Examples:

- police ignoring law duties to help criminals raid the PD
- a hitman killing people without a contract
- a mayor making laws that override server rules
- a player using job changes to dodge active raids, arrests, or contracts
- a shop role only switching jobs to self-supply and immediately switching back

### CORE-008 - No LTARP

LTARP means leaving to avoid roleplay. It includes disconnecting, switching jobs, respawning, killbinding, hiding in spawn, or using menus to escape a live scene.

If you are being raided, arrested, mugged, robbed, kidnapped by a future system, or chased in a valid police scene, finish the scene.

### CORE-009 - Spawn is not a panic room for active scenes

Do not run into spawn or safe areas to avoid a raid, arrest, chase, or valid RP consequence.

Do not start crimes from spawn or camp spawn exits for easy kills.

### CORE-010 - Job abuse is not allowed

Do not switch jobs only to buy restricted equipment, self-supply a group, escape job limits, avoid warrants, clear consequences, or access doors/tools you should not have.

If a mechanic persists through job change, that does not mean job abuse becomes legal.

### CORE-011 - Do not bait rule breaks

You may not trick players into breaking rules so you can report or kill them.

Examples:

- asking someone to kill you without making it clear
- fake adverts that look like valid raid/mug/hit notices
- pretending to be staff
- stepping into crossfire repeatedly to farm reports

### CORE-012 - FearRP is light, not global hard law

There is no strict global FearRP rule by default. Players can choose to fight, run, comply, bargain, or lose.

However, refusing to value your life can still become FailRP if it abuses a specific system or intentionally ruins scenes.

## Mechanical Notes

- NLR is enforced by `Code/Nlr/`.
- Death can drop wallet cash under `docs/death-money-drops.md`.
- Votes and demotions are host-authoritative.
- Staff may still act on revenge/metagaming that mechanics cannot detect.
