# Jobs, Commerce, and Production

## In One Breath

Your job is your permission slip, your costume, and your promise to the server. Do not wear it for five seconds just to grab a toy.

## Rules

### JOB-001 - Play the job you selected

Each job has a role, tools, shop access, salary, and expectations. Use them in a way that makes sense for that job.

Examples:

- Police enforce laws.
- Gun Dealers sell weapons.
- Hitmen use contracts.
- Miners mine and refine.
- Weed Growers grow weed.
- Meth Alchemists run drug machines.

### JOB-002 - No self-supply abuse

Do not switch to a shop role only to buy items for yourself, your organization, or your friends, then immediately switch back.

If the server gives a job special stock access, that job should create roleplay commerce, not a private vending machine.

### JOB-003 - No job switching to dodge consequences

Do not switch jobs to avoid raids, arrests, warrants, NLR, contracts, robbery loss, machine theft, custody, or police attention.

If the scene started while you had the job, finish the scene honestly.

### JOB-004 - High-demand jobs require activity

Jobs with limited slots should be played actively. Staff may move or restrict players who idle in high-demand roles without participating.

Examples:

- Mayor doing no city work.
- Police not responding at all.
- Hitman holding a slot with no contract activity.
- Shop roles occupying slots without selling or interacting.

### JOB-005 - Job tools are not universal permissions

Starting with a weapon or tool does not make every use valid.

Examples:

- A taser does not make random tasing legal.
- A lockpick does not make every door a raid target.
- A sniper rifle does not make every rooftop a hitman contract.

### JOB-006 - Citizen is flexible, not lawless

Citizens may build, shop, work, organize, and roleplay normal life. They do not gain criminal or police authority just because no one stopped them yet.

### JOB-007 - Government jobs cannot run illegal production

Law enforcement and mayoral roles may not own or protect illegal production such as drug labs or illegal contraband operations.

Bitcoin miners are explicitly unavailable to law enforcement by current policy.

### JOB-008 - Gun Dealer and Black Market Dealer must sell responsibly

Weapon sellers should use their role to trade with other players. Do not self-supply, hide shipments from customers while holding the job slot, or use shop access only for your own combat advantage.

Black market stock may be illegal in character. That does not remove server rules around RDM, raids, or scams.

### JOB-009 - Medic, Cook, and service roles should provide service

Support jobs should use their tools to create useful public roleplay. Do not use the job only as a salary skin or combat disguise.

### JOB-010 - Weed Grower and Meth Alchemist are separate roles

Weed Grower owns the weed-growing loop. Meth Alchemist owns meth and drug machine production.

Do not claim old "Drug Dealer" permissions unless the current system grants them.

### JOB-011 - Production machines create risk

Money printers, bitcoin miners, drug machines, weed plants, ore refiners, and similar systems are valuable and visible. Place them responsibly and accept that they can attract thieves, police, and raids.

If you want quiet income, do not build a factory with a trumpet section.

### JOB-012 - Mining and legal work still follow public rules

Legal work does not excuse blocking NPCs, griefing queues, stealing other players' items through exploits, or camping public areas with props and vehicles.

### JOB-013 - Casino and entertainment jobs must be honest about official systems

Use official casino, coinflip, cinema, and TV tools as intended. Do not fake payouts, impersonate official UI, or mislead players about what the system guarantees.

### JOB-014 - Custom job titles are flavor, not new powers

`/job` custom titles do not grant new authority, legal immunity, shop access, police powers, or criminal permissions.

Calling yourself "Secret President" is allowed if it is funny. It does not let you tax people.

## Role Abuse Examples

- Not allowed: switch to Gun Dealer, buy rifles for your crew, switch back to Gangster.
- Not allowed: switch off Hitman because you accepted a difficult contract.
- Allowed: a Gun Dealer sells weapons to criminals and risks police attention.
- Allowed: a Meth Alchemist moves labs after police find the first one.
